🤔 New Here? Let's Break it Down

Click the questions below to see how a quantum device flips the entire universe upside down.

Your phone and Xbox run on standard silicon microchips that read data as simple bits: a switch is either 0 (OFF) or 1 (ON). A quantum computer doesn't use microchips. It traps isolated tiny atoms, cooling them down colder than deep outer space, and programs data directly onto the atom's spin behavior!

Think of a regular data bit as a coin lying flat on a desk—it can only show Heads or Tails. A Qubit is what happens when you spin that coin! While it's spinning in a fast blur, it is holding a fluid mixture of both states at the exact same split-second. This phenomenon is called Superposition, and it lets us process complex codes simultaneously.

Kind of! Quantum mechanics has a rule called Entanglement. If you bind two qubits together, they act like a pair of magical sync-linked dice. If you roll one here in Maryland and hit a 6, your friend's die on the other side of the planet instantly turns into a 6 without a single wire connecting them. We use this connection to make hacker-proof encryption loops!
