🔮 The Rules of the Quantum Universe

In our everyday world, things are simple: a light switch is either ON or OFF. But at the atomic level, the universe starts acting like a simulator with cheat codes enabled. Here are the core laws we use to compute:

1. Superposition (The Spinning Coin Analogy)

A coin flat on a table is either "Heads" or "Tails" (0 or 1). But a spinning coin is a liquid blur of both states simultaneously. It only chooses a side when you stop it. In quantum computing, our data bits spin in a state called a Qubit, allowing us to evaluate countless combinations all at once.

2. Entanglement (The Teleporting Dice Analogy)

Imagine two cosmic dice. You roll yours here in Howard County and hit a 6; your teammate's die halfway around the planet instantly snaps to a 6 at that exact split-second. We leverage this deep physical connection to tie data streams together into un-hackable cryptography links.
