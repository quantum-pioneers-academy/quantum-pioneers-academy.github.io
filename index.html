🤔 New Here? Let's Break it Down

Click the questions below to see how a quantum device flips the entire universe upside down.

Standard electronics run on regular silicon microchips that read data as simple bits: a path is either strictly 0 (OFF) or strictly 1 (ON). A quantum computer doesn't use microchips. Instead, it traps tiny single atoms, freezes them colder than outer space, and programs data directly onto how the atoms vibrate and spin!

Think of a regular data bit as a coin resting flat on a desk—it's locked as either Heads or Tails. A Qubit is what happens when you spin that coin! While it's spinning in a fast blur, it holds a fluid mixture of both states at the exact same split-second. This phenomenon is called Superposition, and it lets us process thousands of complex data paths simultaneously.

Yes! Quantum mechanics has a rule called Entanglement. If you bind two qubits together, they act like a pair of magical sync-linked game items. If you change the state of one here in Maryland, its linked partner on the other side of the planet instantly updates to match it without a single wire or network path connecting them. We use this connection to build hacker-proof encryption setups!
